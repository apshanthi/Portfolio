About

As an Airworthiness Limitation Specialist, I bridge the gap between aeronautical engineering and in-service aircraft maintenance. My role is to ensure long-term aircraft safety and regulatory compliance by analyzing maintenance data, managing structural integrity programs, and defining airworthiness limitations.

My foundation is a Manufacturing Engineering degree from McMaster University, which provides a unique "design for manufacturing" perspective on why these limitations exist. This is enhanced by my practical experience as a Data Analyst at CAMP Systems, where I developed expertise in leveraging maintenance program data to drive reliability and safety decisions.

I am a hands-on engineer driven to solve complex problems at the intersection of manufacturing, maintenance, and data analysis.
